My Family
Migrations

Six journeys across time, continents and memory

Migration is any displacement that involves a change of permanent residence, whether across borders, regions, or provinces. My family's story spans more than a century of movement: from the great transatlantic voyages of the early twentieth century to today's graduate students crossing European cities with a laptop and a suitcase. Each journey was shaped by its era's politics, economics, and hopes. Together, they form a living atlas of Spanish — and human — migration.

The Timeline of Journeys

c. 1912  ·  Transoceanic Wave
Manuel Jiménez García
Great-great-grandfather · Bisabuelo
Cáceres, Extremadura → Buenos Aires, Argentina 🇦🇷
Aged around 22, Manuel left during the wave of Spanish emigration to the River Plate. He worked on a cattle estancia in the province of Buenos Aires and later opened a small grocery with fellow emigrants from Extremadura. He never returned.
Voluntary International Permanent Transoceanic Migration
⛵
🌾
c. 1952  ·  Rural Exodus
Rosario Rivero Campos
Great-grandmother · Bisabuela
Malpartida de Cáceres → Madrid, Comunidad de Madrid 🇪🇸
Widowed at 38, Rosario moved with her three children to Madrid's working-class district of Vallecas. She found work as a domestic servant (empleada del hogar). The family never returned to live in Extremadura, though they visited every summer. Her move was part of the massive rural exodus that depopulated inland Spain.
Voluntary National Permanent Rural Exodus (Éxodo Rural)
1964  ·  Guest Worker Era
Francisco & Carmen Jiménez
Great-uncle & great-aunt · Tatarabuelo político
Navalmoral de la Mata, Cáceres → Stuttgart, Germany 🇩🇪
Francisco signed a contract through the bilateral agreement between Spain and Germany in 1964. He worked on an assembly line at a car plant (Daimler-Benz), while Carmen cleaned in a hotel. They lived in a workers' hostel (Wohnheim) for two years before renting a flat. They retired to Spain in 1986.
Voluntary International Temporary (22 yrs) Migration to Western Europe
🏭
🏗️
1983  ·  Post-Transition Spain
Rafael Jiménez Moreno
Grandfather · Abuelo
Cáceres city → Barcelona, Catalonia 🇪🇸
After the Spanish democratic transition, Rafael followed construction work to Barcelona's booming building sector. Aged 28, he moved alone first, then brought his family a year later. He became a foreman (encargado de obra) and retired in Hospitalet de Llobregat. He never returned permanently to Extremadura.
Voluntary National (interregional) Permanent Internal Economic Migration
2004  ·  EU Free Movement
Lucía Rivero Sánchez
Aunt · Tía
Cáceres city → London, United Kingdom 🇬🇧
With a degree in Tourism from the University of Extremadura, Lucía moved to London after Spain's entry into the Schengen zone had matured. She worked in hotel management in the City and Canary Wharf. After Brexit she obtained indefinite leave to remain. She lives in Brixton, visits family twice a year, and considers London her home.
Voluntary International Permanent Contemporary Skilled Migration
✈️
🎓
2022  ·  Present Day
Sofía Jiménez Torres
Cousin · Prima
Cáceres city → Seville, Andalusia 🇪🇸
Sofía moved to Seville at 18 to study Architecture. She lives in a shared flat (piso compartido) in the Triana district. She returns to Cáceres during holiday breaks. Her migration illustrates the current attraction of large Spanish cities — especially Seville, Madrid, and Barcelona — for students from smaller Extremaduran towns and cities.
Voluntary National (interregional) Temporary (student) Contemporary Student Migration
